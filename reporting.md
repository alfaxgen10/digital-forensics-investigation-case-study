# Case Study – Forensic Reporting

## Objective
To document the forensic investigation in a structured, professional report that summarizes evidence, analysis, and conclusions while maintaining chain of custody.

## Report Structure
1. **Executive Summary**
   - Provided a high-level overview of the incident and investigation scope.
   - Highlighted key findings and their impact.

2. **Evidence Summary**
   - Listed all acquired evidence (RAM dumps, HDD images, network captures).
   - Included hash values and chain of custody documentation.

3. **Analysis**
   - Detailed findings from memory, disk, and network analysis.
   - Presented malware identification results and IOC validation.
   - Correlated evidence across multiple sources to reconstruct the attack scenario.

4. **Conclusions**
   - Confirmed compromise through malicious processes and persistence mechanisms.
   - Validated findings with external threat intelligence (VirusTotal).
   - Outlined the reconstructed timeline of events.

5. **Recommendations**
   - Suggested incident response actions (patching, malware removal, network monitoring).
   - Proposed improvements to evidence handling and forensic readiness.

## Chain of Custody
- Documented acquisition steps, timestamps, and responsible analysts.
- Ensured evidence integrity through hash validation.
- Maintained logs for transparency and admissibility.

## Deliverables
- Formal forensic report (structured document).
- Supporting evidence files and screenshots.
- IOC list and reconstructed incident timeline.
